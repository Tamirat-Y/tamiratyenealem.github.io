Lightning Strike Data Analysis

An in-depth analysis was conducted on lightning strike data from 2016 to 2018, using over 13 million records sourced from NOAA. Spatial and temporal trends were examined, revealing consistent lightning hotspots in Cuba and the southern U.S., with peak activity during summer months. Visualizations were generated to highlight seasonal patterns and high-risk areas across 170,000+ unique locations. These insights support disaster preparedness, climate research, and weather-related infrastructure planning.
